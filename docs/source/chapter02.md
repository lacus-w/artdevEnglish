# Chapter 2: Taking Notes Without Losing Your Mind

> **Core mindset:**  
> Notes are not criticism of your skill; they are data about the **product**.  
> Your job is to **translate** notes into better design, not to **defend** your ego.

---

## 1. Opening Scenario

**Setting:**  
Mid-week dailies. You present a polished prop concept for the hero's weapon. You're proud of the detail and the rendering.

**Art Director squints at the screen:**
> "Hmm. Interesting. The shapes are a bit busy. Maybe simplify? Also, I'm not getting a strong read on the blade. Let's try a few more passes."

**Your internal monologue:**  
*Interesting? Is that good or bad? Busy how? Simplify which parts? And how many 'passes' does he want? Infinite?*

---

## 2. Survival Phrase List

### A. Clarifying Vague Notes（把模糊反馈"钉"实）

- "To make sure I understood—should I focus on **silhouette readability** first, then re-add details?"
- "When you say 'busy,' are you referring to the **surface ornamentation** or the **primary shapes**?"
- "So the priority is **graphic clarity over realism**—did I get that right?"
- "Are we looking to **push the contrast** between the blade and the hilt?"

### B. Managing Scope & Revisions（管理改稿次数）

- "I can do **one more exploratory pass** within the current scope."
- "To really solve the silhouette, I'd suggest **rolling back to greyscale** rather than polishing this version."
- "I'll explore **two distinct directions**: one conservative, one pushing the brief further."
- "Just to set expectations, I'll aim to have the revised passes ready for **Friday's dailies**."

### C. Handling "Kill Shots"（应对致命反馈）

- "Noted. I'll **archive this thread** and start a fresh exploration based on these new points."
- "Understood. I'll treat this as **blue-sky research** rather than a production piece for now."

## 3. Subtext Decoder

| What They Say | What They (Probably) Mean | How to Respond |
|---------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| "Interesting..." | I don't like it, but I'm polite. | "I'm hearing this isn't quite landing. Should I pivot the shape language?" |
| "It's a bit safe." | Be more creative; surprise me. | "Got it. I'll push the silhouette further outside the box on the next pass." |
| "Let's park this for now." | It's dead. Don't bring it back unless asked. | "Understood. I'll move it to the archive folder." |
| "Can you just...?" | This is a small task in their head (it never is). | "Sure, I can look at that. How would you like me to **re-prioritize** my current tasks?" |
| "Wow, you're fast!" | You delivered too fast and skipped thinking. Or they're setting you up for more work. | "Thanks! I focused on the structure first. I'll slow down to ensure the next pass has stronger design logic." |

**Pro Insight:**  
In AAA, **"Read"** (可读性) is king. If they say "I'm not getting a read," they mean the silhouette, values, or color separation failed—not that your brushwork is bad.

## 4. Micro-Roleplay

**Context:** You presented a character sketch. The AD gives notes.

**AD:**  
"The pose feels a bit stiff. And maybe bring the saturation down a touch?"

**Level 1 — Passive:**  
"Okay."  
→ *You didn't clarify what "stiff" means or how much to desaturate.*

**Level 2 — Professional:**  
"Will do. I'll loosen up the gesture and pull the saturation back by about 10%."  
→ *Better, but still just taking orders.*

**Level 3 — Designer Mindset:**  
"Noted. To fix the stiffness, I'll push the contrapposto and exaggerate the shoulder line. Regarding saturation, I'll mute the base armor to make the accent colors pop—does that align with the visual hierarchy we want?"  
→ *You explained **why** and **how**, demonstrating expertise.*

## 5. Common Mistakes

| 中文直觉 | 中式英语 (Avoid) | 行业英语 (Recommended) |
|----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| "我不觉得这里乱啊。" | I don't think it's messy. | I understand the concern about complexity. I can simplify the secondary forms to improve the read. |
| "我回去改。" | I will go back to change it. | I'll iterate on this. I'll take this into the next pass. |
| "你到底想要什么？" | What do you want? | What are the top three priorities for this revision? / Which direction should I lean into? |
| "这个很难画。" | This is hard to draw. | This shape presents a **clarity challenge** at this scale; I recommend simplifying the geometry. |

## 6. Pipeline Snapshot

**The Feedback Loop (Visualized):**

```
1. Block-in / Thumbnails  →  "Explore. Don't commit."
2. Rough Pass             →  "Lock the silhouette. Ignore details."
3. Tight Pass             →  "Refine edges, values, and materials."
4. Final Polish           →  "Only after the AD says 'Lock it.'"
```

**Critical Rule:**  
Never polish a **Rough Pass**. If you do, you're being "precious" about your work.

**Useful phrase:**  
"I'll keep this unpolished so we can focus purely on the design."

**Jira Comment Example:**

> **Update v2:** Addressed notes from today's dailies.  
> - Simplified secondary shapes on the pauldrons to reduce tangents.  
> - Pushed value contrast on the blade to improve readability against the background.  
> - Kept rendering loose as per our focus on design stage.  
> *Ready for another look when you have a moment.*

## 7. The "Paintover" Etiquette

Being paintover-ed is a rite of passage. How you react matters more than the paint-over itself.

**If you receive a paintover:**

- ✅ **DO:** "Thanks for the paintover—this clarifies the mass shift I was missing. I'll integrate this into the next pass."
- ❌ **DON'T:** "But I spent three hours on those details!" (They don't care about your hours; they care about the result.)

**If you want to request a paintover:**

- "I'm struggling to visualize the volume here. Would you be open to a quick paintover to show the planes you're looking for?"

## 8. Homework

1. **Translate this scenario:**  
   Your Lead says: "It's fine, but maybe try something a bit more... iconic?"  
   Write a response that **clarifies** what "iconic" means in the context of shape language.

2. **Roleplay Prep:**  
   Prepare a 30-second script explaining why you are **reverting to greyscale** to fix a design, even though the AD asked for "more color."

<details>
<summary>Answer Key</summary>

1. **Response:** "When you say 'iconic,' are we talking about a bolder silhouette, a unique material treatment, or a specific cultural motif? I want to make sure I'm pushing the right lever."

2. **Script:** "I hear the note on color, but before adding more hue, I want to ensure the **value structure** holds up. If the form isn't readable in greyscale, no amount of color will save it. I'll lock the values first, then apply the color pass to enhance the hierarchy."
</details>
