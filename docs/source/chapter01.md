# Chapter 1: Onboarding Without Getting Owned

> **Core mindset:**  
> Onboarding isn't about proving you're talented. It's about proving you're **low maintenance**.

---

## 1. Opening Scenario

**Setting:**  
Monday morning. You've just joined a new studio as a mid-level Concept Artist. You're added to seven Slack channels, three Google Drives, and a Jira project called *Project Chimera*. Your Lead says:

> "Check the drive, skim the bible, and let me know if anything's unclear."

**Your internal panic:**  
*Which drive? Which bible? How deep do I need to read? And what if I ask a stupid question?*

---

## 2. Survival Phrase List

### Getting Oriented（找方向）

- "Where can I find the **latest style guide / art bible**?"
- "Is there a **file naming convention** I should follow?"
- "Am I supposed to work off the **master PSDs** or start fresh?"

### Clarifying Expectations（确认预期）

- "Just to clarify—should I focus on **exploration** or **polish** first?"
- "How tight do you want the first pass?"
- "Do you want this **paint-over friendly**?"

### Managing Your WIP（管理半成品）

- "I'll keep this in a **WIP state** until we lock the silhouette."
- "I'll mark this as **exploratory** so it doesn't get mistaken for final."
- "I'll drop a **quick grey-box version** to test the read."

---

## 3. Subtext Decoder

| What They Say | What They (Probably) Mean |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| "Skim the bible." | Don't spend two days reading—get the tone, the rules, and the no-nos. |
| "Keep it loose." | Don't polish. We might throw this away. |
| "Run with it." | I trust you—don't come back every 20 minutes for approval. |
| "Let me know if anything's unclear." | I won't explain everything twice—ask smart questions now. |

**Pro tip:**  
"Unclear" ≠ "I don't understand."  
Better: "I noticed X contradicts Y—should I prioritize X?"

---

## 4. Micro-Roleplay

**Context:** You're in your first Teams call with your Art Lead.

**Lead:**  
"Take a look at the environment deck in the Drive. We need some quick explorations for the hub area."

**Option A — Junior move:**  
"Okay, cool."  
→ *Too vague. You haven't clarified scope or expectations.*

**Option B — Survival move:**  
"Got it. Just to confirm—should I focus on **big shapes and mood** first, or go straight into architectural detailing?"  
→ *Shows you think in production terms.*

**Option C — High-level move:**  
"Roger. I'll do a **grey-box + value pass** to lock readability, then explore two contrasting moods. I'll check in once I have 2–3 rough directions."  
→ *This is how seniors sound.*

## 5. Common Mistakes

| 中文直觉 | 中式英语 (Avoid) | 行业英语 (Recommended) |
|----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| "我会读一下。" | I will read it. | I'll go through the deck and flag any questions. |
| "这图还没做完。" | This picture is not finished. | This is still a WIP—silhouette locked, details pending. |
| "你要我画什么？" | What do you want me to draw? | What's the priority for this pass—mood, structure, or narrative? |
| "我觉得这样不错。" | I think this is good. | This approach supports readability and fits the narrative beat. |

## 6. Pipeline Snapshot

**Typical Day 1–2 Folder Structure:**

```text
Project_Chimera/
├── _REF/
│   ├── Style_Guide_v4.pdf
│   └── World_Bible.pptx
├── ENV/
│   ├── HUB/
│   │   ├── WIP_YourName/
│   │   └── _Approved/
├── CHARACTER/
└── _Templates/
    └── Concept_Template.psd
```

**Key takeaway:**  
If you don't see a `_Templates` folder, **ask**. Using the wrong canvas size or color profile is a fast way to lose trust.
